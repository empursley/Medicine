# Copy & Language Guidelines (Landing Page)

## Objective
All text on this landing page must communicate **clarity, credibility, and professionalism**.  
The tone should reflect a **high-value SaaS product sold to medical offices**, not a casual consumer app.

We are correcting:
- Unprofessional wording
- Vague or confusing language
- Overly casual or “startup hype” tone

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Clear > Clever
- Use simple, direct language
- Avoid metaphors, jokes, or slang
- Every sentence should be immediately understood on first read

Bad:
> “Never lose track of your health journey again!”

Good:
> “Keep every doctor visit, note, and medication organized in one place.”

---

### 2. Concise & Efficient
- Remove filler words
- Avoid long paragraphs
- Each line should deliver a single idea

Bad:
> “Our platform is designed to help patients better manage and organize their healthcare experience in a more effective and streamlined way.”

Good:
> “Organize appointments, notes, and medications in one system.”

---

### 3. Professional, Not Clinical
- Sound trustworthy and polished
- Avoid overly technical medical jargon
- Avoid sounding like a hospital or research paper

Goal tone:
- SaaS product for professionals
- Not a medical journal
- Not a casual app

---

### 4. High-Ticket Positioning
This product is sold to **offices and providers**, not just individuals.

Language should:
- Emphasize reliability and structure
- Suggest operational improvement
- Avoid sounding cheap or “consumer app-like”

Bad:
> “Super easy app to help your patients stay on track!”

Good:
> “A structured system that helps patients retain and act on clinical information.”

---

### 5. No Overpromising
- Do NOT claim outcomes that aren’t guaranteed
- Avoid statements implying medical accuracy or compliance unless explicitly confirmed

Avoid:
- “Ensures patients never forget anything”
- “Improves medical outcomes”

Use:
- “Helps patients retain key information”
- “Designed to reduce missed details after visits”

---

### 6. Consistent Voice
All sections must feel like they are written by the same entity:
- Calm
- Controlled
- Confident
- Direct

Avoid:
- Switching between casual and formal tone
- Marketing hype mixed with technical language

---

## Formatting Rules

- Use short paragraphs (1–2 sentences)
- Prefer bullet points for features/benefits
- Use strong, direct headlines
- Avoid excessive punctuation (!!!, …)
- Avoid emojis

---

## Word Choice Guidelines

### Preferred Words
- organize
- track
- manage
- record
- access
- review
- structured
- clear
- consistent

### Avoid
- “game-changing”
- “revolutionary”
- “insane”
- “crazy powerful”
- “super easy”
- “life-changing”

---

## Section-Specific Guidance

### Hero Section
- One clear value proposition
- One supporting sentence
- No fluff

### Features
- Focus on function, not hype
- Explain what it does, not why it’s amazing

### Benefits
- Translate features into practical outcomes
- Keep realistic and grounded

### CTA
- Direct and professional
- Example:
  - “Request a Demo”
  - “See How It Works”
  - NOT: “Get Started Now!!!”

---

## Final Standard

Before approving any text, ask:

1. Is this immediately understandable?
2. Does this sound credible to a medical office?
3. Is this concise?
4. Does this avoid hype or exaggeration?

If any answer is no → rewrite.

---

## Summary

The landing page should feel like:
> A structured, high-quality SaaS product built for professionals

NOT:
> A flashy startup or consumer app

Clarity, restraint, and professionalism take priority over creativity.
